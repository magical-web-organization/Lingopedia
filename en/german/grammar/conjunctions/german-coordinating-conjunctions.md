# German Coordinating Conjunctions

Coordinating conjunctions are one of the most essential elements of German grammar. These connectors link two separate clauses or sentences, thereby enhancing their meaning or conveying an additional thought or idea.

In the German language, there are seven coordinating conjunctions or *Konjunktionen*:

- **und** (and)
- **aber** (but)
- **oder** (or)
- **denn** (for, because)
- **sondern** (but rather)
- **doch** (yet, still)
- **nicht nur ... sondern auch** (not only ... but also)

## Use of German Coordinating Conjunctions

The use of coordinating conjunctions in German is somewhat similar to the English language. They are used to connect words, phrases, clauses, and sentences.

Here are a few examples of how to use German coordinating conjunctions:

### Und (and)

*Seine Schwester ist Ärztin, und sein Bruder ist Lehrer.*

(His sister is a doctor, and his brother is a teacher.)

### Aber (but)

*Ich bin müde, aber ich muss noch arbeiten.*

(I'm tired, but I still have to work.)

### Oder (or)

*Willst du Kaffee oder Tee?*

(Do you want coffee or tea?)

### Denn (for, because)

*Ich kann nicht ausgehen, denn ich habe zu viel Arbeit.*

(I can't go out because I have too much work.)

### Sondern (but rather)

*Er ist nicht krank, sondern er hat einfach keinen Appetit.*

(He isn't sick, but rather he simply has no appetite.)

### Doch (yet, still)

*Ich dachte, ich hätte mein Handy verloren, doch es war in meiner Tasche.*

(I thought I had lost my phone, yet it was in my bag.)

### Nicht nur ... sondern auch (not only ... but also)

*Er ist nicht nur ein guter Schüler, sondern auch ein guter Sportler.*

(He is not only a good student but also a good athlete.)

## Conclusion

Coordinating conjunctions occupy an important place in German language learning. Understanding their use and meaning can help you express your ideas and thoughts more effectively while speaking or writing in German.
