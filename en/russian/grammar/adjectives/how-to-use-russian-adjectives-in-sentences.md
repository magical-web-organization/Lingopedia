Russian adjectives are words that describe or modify nouns in a sentence. Unlike English adjectives, Russian adjectives have different forms depending on their function in the sentence, gender, and case.

In this article, we will explore how to use Russian adjectives in sentences. We will discuss the different forms of Russian adjectives, how to use gender agreement, and word order. 

### Basic Forms of Russian Adjectives

Russian adjectives can have three different forms: masculine, feminine, and neuter. These forms depend on the gender of the noun that they modify.

For example, the adjective "beautiful" in Russian is красивый (krasiviy). The masculine form красивый (krasiviy) is used with masculine nouns: 

- Красивый дом (krasiviy dom) - "beautiful house"

The feminine form красивая (krasivaya) is used with feminine nouns: 

- Красивая машина (krasivaya mashina) - "beautiful car"

The neuter form красивое (krasivoe) is used with neuter nouns:

- Красивое платье (krasivoe platye) - "beautiful dress"

### Adjective Declension

Like many other parts of Russian grammar, adjectives are declined. This means that their ending changes depending on the case of the noun they modify. 

For example, the word красивый (krasiviy) would decline as follows:

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |

| Nominative | красивый (krasiviy) | красивая (krasivaya) | красивое (krasivoe) |
| Genitive | красивого (krasivogo) | красивой (krasivoy) | красивого (krasivogo) |
| Dative | красивому (krasivomu) | красивой (krasivoy) | красивому (krasivomu) |

### Gender Agreement

In Russian, the adjective must agree with the noun it modifies in both gender and number. This means that if the noun is masculine and plural, the adjective must also be masculine and plural. 

For example:

- Красивый дом и красивые цветы (krasiviy dom i krasivye tsvety) - "beautiful house and beautiful flowers"

- Красивая женщина и красивые платья (krasivaya zhenshchina i krasivye platya) - "beautiful woman and beautiful dresses"

### Word Order

In Russian, the word order can be flexible. However, the most common word order in a Russian sentence is Subject-Verb-Object-Adjective. 

For example: 

- Я вижу красивую картину (ya vizhu krasivuyu kartinu) - "I see a beautiful picture"

In this sentence, the subject (я - ya) comes first, followed by the verb (вижу - vizhu), object (картину - kartinu), and adjective (красивую - krasivuyu).
